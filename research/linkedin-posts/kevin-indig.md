## Post Theme 1: "The Fan-Out Effect in AI Search"

**Date Range:** Q1 2026
**Source:** LinkedIn / Growth Memo

Kevin describes how AI Mode triggers "fan-out" queries — where one user prompt leads to multiple behind-the-scenes searches by the AI. He advises brands to aim for visibility in these conversational query variations rather than focusing solely on head terms.

> "AI doesn't search like humans do. One prompt triggers dozens of internal queries. If you're only optimizing for the keywords users type, you're missing the full picture."

**Key takeaway:** Optimize for the full "query constellation" that AI creates behind the scenes, not just the user-facing prompt.

## Post Theme 2: "Shorter Content Wins in AI Citations"

**Date Range:** Late 2025
**Source:** LinkedIn

Kevin's analysis of AI citation patterns reveals that shorter, focused content often outperforms exhaustive, broad guides in AI citations. He advocates for regularly updated content, as freshness is a key driver for LLM visibility.

**Key takeaway:** Shift from exhaustive pillar pages to focused, regularly refreshed content designed for extraction by AI models.

## Post Theme 3: "From Clicks to Influence — The New Measurement Framework"

**Date Range:** Q4 2025
**Source:** LinkedIn / Growth Memo

Kevin emphasizes that AI Overviews and LLM responses are probabilistic rather than deterministic. This makes traditional ranking measurement less reliable. Brands should track "influence" metrics — citation rates, mention frequency, and brand positioning — across various LLMs.

> "Stop obsessing over position #1. Start tracking how often you're cited across ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Gemini. That's the new leaderboard."

**Key takeaway:** Build a measurement framework around AI citations and brand influence, not just SERP position tracking.

## Post Theme 4: "Community as a Signal — Reddit and YouTube Matter"

**Date Range:** Q1 2026
**Source:** LinkedIn

Platforms like Reddit and YouTube are consistently identified as top-cited domains in AI responses. Kevin encourages brands to establish a strong, authentic presence in these communities, as users often validate synthetic AI answers against human-generated social proof.

> "Reddit isn't just a community platform anymore. It's one of the most-cited domains in AI search. If your brand isn't present there authentically, you're invisible to the models that matter."

**Key takeaway:** Invest in authentic community presence on Reddit and YouTube — these platforms are critical inputs for LLM training and citation.

## Post Theme 5: "Breaking Silos — SEO as Organizational Translation"

**Date Range:** Early 2026
**Source:** LinkedIn

Kevin argues that AI SEO transformation often fails at the "alignment layer" rather than the tactics layer. SEO, PR, content, and social media teams must break down silos to coordinate messaging and maximize offsite signals. He advocates for SEOs to become "organizational translators" who communicate the AI search mandate to leadership in business impact terms.

> "The biggest blocker to AI visibility isn't your content. It's your org chart. SEO, PR, content, and social can't operate in silos anymore."

**Key takeaway:** Modern SEO is an organizational challenge, not just a tactical one. SEOs need to bridge teams and speak the language of business impact.
