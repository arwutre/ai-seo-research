## Post Theme 1: "AI Visibility is the New SEO KPI"

**Date Range:** Q4 2025 – Q1 2026
**Source:** LinkedIn / Siege Media

Ross argues that the traditional "rank → CTR → traffic" model is breaking down due to AI Overviews and LLMs. The focus must shift from generic top-of-funnel "what is" content to bottom-of-funnel money pages, comparison articles, pricing pages, templates, and calculators.

> "If your SEO strategy still revolves around 'what is X' blog posts, you're building for a world that no longer exists. AI answers those. Focus on the pages that convert."

**Key takeaway:** Shift SEO investment from TOFU educational content (being eaten by AI) to BOFU conversion-oriented assets.

---

## Post Theme 2: "Data Journalism as a Defensible Moat"

**Date Range:** Late 2025
**Source:** LinkedIn / Siege Media research

Ross emphasizes that original data studies remain a highly defensible strategy. Creating unique data, repurposing those findings across multiple assets, and using them to support opinionated thought leadership provides value that commoditized AI-generated content cannot replicate.

> "AI can write a 2,000-word blog post in 30 seconds. It can't conduct a survey of 1,000 marketers and extract novel insights. That's your moat."

**Key takeaway:** Invest in proprietary research and data journalism — this is the content type most resistant to AI commoditization.

## Post Theme 3: "The 'Crash-the-Party' Strategy"

**Date Range:** 2025
**Source:** LinkedIn / Siege Media

A key tactic Ross discusses is creating "crash-the-party" comparison pages designed to capture high-intent demand by positioning a brand directly against competitors. This is a format LLMs often cite and users actively seek.

> "The best BOFU content inserts your brand into existing buying conversations. Comparison pages are the most undervalued content type in B2B SaaS."

**Key takeaway:** Create competitive comparison content that positions your brand in existing purchase decisions — highly cited by both LLMs and users.

---

## Post Theme 4: "Traffic is No Longer the Health Metric"

**Date Range:** Q1 2026
**Source:** LinkedIn

With AI changing how users interact with search, Ross advises marketers to prioritize metrics like demo requests, pipeline, deal size, and overall organic channel performance over raw traffic volume.

> "Reporting traffic numbers to your CEO in 2026 is like reporting page views in 2010. It tells you nothing about business impact. Track pipeline. Track revenue. That's what matters."

**Key takeaway:** Align SEO metrics with business outcomes (pipeline, revenue) rather than vanity metrics (traffic, rankings).

---

## Post Theme 5: "AI for Efficiency, Not Replacement"

**Date Range:** 2025–2026
**Source:** LinkedIn / Siege Media

At Siege Media, AI is used as a "sparring partner" and for automation tasks like keyword research and content refreshes, but not to replace high-quality human-driven expertise. Ross maintains that outstanding, differentiated content is necessary to succeed in a market where AI can easily generate "mediocre" content.

> "We use AI to make our people faster, not to replace them. The bar for content quality just got higher because everyone has access to 'good enough.'"

**Key takeaway:** Use AI to accelerate expert-driven workflows, but maintain human expertise as the differentiator.
