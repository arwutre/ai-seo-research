## Post Theme 1: "AEO is Not SEO 2.0 — Stop Rebranding Fundamentals"

**Date Range:** Q4 2025
**Source:** LinkedIn / Newsletter "The Future of SEO"

Eli is skeptical of the "Answer Engine Optimization" (AEO) branding, viewing it as a buzzword invented to sell services. He argues the fundamentals haven't changed — understanding user intent, providing genuine value, and building authority remain the most important factors.

> "AEO isn't new. It's what good SEO has always been — matching content to intent and proving you're the best source. We just have a new acronym to justify new consulting fees."

**Key takeaway:** Don't chase buzzwords. Focus on the timeless SEO fundamentals — user intent, value, and authority — regardless of what the industry calls it.

## Post Theme 2: "The Mid-Funnel is Where SEO Wins Now"

**Date Range:** Q1 2026
**Source:** LinkedIn

Eli emphasizes that because AI models dominate top-of-funnel queries, SEO strategy should increasingly focus on mid-funnel users who are seeking options, comparisons, and solutions where they can evaluate products or services.

> "AI owns the 'what is' space. Your opportunity is the 'which one should I choose' space. That's where intent meets action, and that's where SEO drives revenue."

**Key takeaway:** Shift focus from top-of-funnel educational content to mid-funnel evaluation content where purchase decisions happen.

## Post Theme 3: "Stop Spamming Reddit for AI Citations"

**Date Range:** Early 2026
**Source:** LinkedIn / productledseo.com

Eli warns that attempting to game platforms like Reddit for AI citations is not a viable long-term strategy. Instead, focus on earning authentic mentions and building genuine community presence.

> "Buying old Reddit accounts to seed mentions of your product isn't SEO strategy. It's spam. And LLMs will get better at detecting it, just like Google got better at detecting link spam."

**Key takeaway:** Invest in authentic community participation rather than trying to game AI training data through manufactured mentions.

---

## Post Theme 4: "SEO Teams Need PRDs, Not 'SEO Favors'"

**Date Range:** 2025
**Source:** LinkedIn

Eli advocates for SEO teams to work more closely with engineering via Product Requirement Documents (PRDs) rather than just requesting ad-hoc "SEO favors." This elevates SEO from a marketing afterthought to a product-level priority.

> "If your SEO strategy requires asking engineers for 'favors,' you've already lost. Write PRDs. Get on the product roadmap. That's how Product-Led SEO works."

**Key takeaway:** Treat SEO as a product function with formal engineering requirements, not a marketing bolt-on.

## Post Theme 5: "Control Your AEO with Your Own Website"

**Date Range:** Q1 2026
**Source:** LinkedIn / productledseo.com

Eli focuses on ensuring your own owned properties remain the primary source of truth for AI engines. Rather than chasing external platforms, invest in making your website the most authoritative, structured, and complete source on your topics.

> "The best way to influence what AI says about your brand is to make your website the definitive source. Schema markup, clear structure, complete topical coverage — control the narrative at the source."

**Key takeaway:** Your owned website is your primary lever for AI visibility — invest in making it the authoritative source AI models trust.
