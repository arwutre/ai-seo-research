## The Problem This Playbook Solves

B2B SaaS companies are losing organic traffic to AI Overviews and LLM-powered search. The old playbook — write long-form educational content, rank #1, get clicks — is breaking down. AI answers "what is" questions directly. TOFU traffic is collapsing.

At the same time, the solution is NOT to chase GEO buzzwords or generate AI content at scale and hope for the best. This playbook gives a structured, evidence-based system for content that performs in both traditional search AND AI-driven discovery — without gambling your brand on tactics that will be penalized in 12 months.

---

## Strategic Foundation: How AI Constructs Answers

Before any tactic, understand the architecture. Bernard Huang breaks AI response generation into three layers:

1. **Training data layer** — what the model learned before its knowledge cutoff
2. **Validation layer** — real-time web searches the AI performs to fact-check its answer before responding
3. **Memory & context layer** — personalization from past user conversations

*(Bernard Huang, [youtube.com/watch?v=2Ldce9z_ZuM](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2Ldce9z_ZuM), AEO Playbook Webinar, collected 22.04.2026)*

**Your leverage points are layers 1 and 2.** Training data moves slowly (months-long update cycles). The validation layer is where you can win fast — by publishing content that intercepts the AI's real-time research queries before your competitors do.

## Part 1: Core Recommendations & SOP

### STEP 1 — Audit Your Current AI Visibility Baseline

Before creating any content, establish where you stand.

**Actions:**
1. Open ChatGPT and Gemini. Ask: *"What can you tell me about [Your Brand]?"* and *"What are the best tools for [your category]?"* Screenshot every response.
2. Use a prompt tracking tool — Ross Hudgens recommends **Peak** or **Profound** — to systematically test which commercial prompts mention you vs. competitors.
3. Use **Ahrefs Brand Radar** to identify which AI keywords currently mention you and which mention competitors only.
4. Note which specific pages on your site appear as AI citations. These are your "anchor pages" — protect and refresh them first.

**Why:** "You need to figure out all the AI opportunities in your niche so you can target them and optimize for them." *(Matt Diggity, [youtube.com/watch?v=4GBlHObjOrY](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4GBlHObjOrY), collected 22.04.2026)*

**KPI to establish:** Number of commercial prompts in which your brand appears, per week. This is your baseline.

---

### STEP 2 — Map the "Fan-Out" Validation Query Constellation

When a user prompts an AI, the model doesn't just search that one phrase. It fans out into 10–30 sub-queries to validate its answer. Most of these have near-zero monthly search volume — traditional keyword tools miss them entirely.

**Actions:**
1. Open **Google AI Studio** (free) with Gemini Flash, Thinking Mode on, and Grounding with Google Search enabled.
2. Type your core commercial prompt (e.g., *"What is the best project management software for agencies?"*).
3. When the response finishes, scroll to the bottom — Gemini shows you the exact web searches it performed to build the answer. Screenshot and copy every query.
4. Repeat for your top 10–15 commercial prompts.
5. Cluster the resulting 100–300 fan-out queries by topic. Each cluster becomes a content brief.

**Why:** "Instead of targeting what users are researching with monthly search volume, we're targeting what agents are thinking when creating their responses for prompts." *(Bernard Huang, [youtube.com/watch?v=2Ldce9z_ZuM](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2Ldce9z_ZuM), collected 22.04.2026)*

**Why 2:** "One prompt triggers dozens of internal queries. If you're only optimizing for the keywords users type, you're missing the full picture." *(Kevin Indig, [linkedin.com/in/kevinindig](https://www.linkedin.com/in/kevinindig/), post: "The Fan-Out Effect in AI Search", Q1 2026, collected 22.04.2026)*

---

### STEP 3 — Restructure Your Content Mix

Ross Hudgens analyzed ~200 client websites. Finding: "what is" and "how-to" content saw the steepest traffic declines; comparison, pricing, and transactional content held up or grew.

**Content priority stack (highest ROI → lowest):**

| Priority | Content Type | Why It Survives AI |
|----------|-------------|-------------------|
| 🔴 Critical | Competitor comparison pages | Users want opinions AI won't give |
| 🔴 Critical | Pricing & true cost guides | AI avoids committing to specific prices |
| 🟠 High | Templates and interactive calculators | Functional — can't be replaced by a chat response |
| 🟠 High | Case studies with real metrics | First-party data AI cannot fabricate |
| 🟡 Medium | Integration and feature-specific pages | Long-tail, low-competition, high intent |
| 🟢 Maintain | Core educational content | Needed for topical authority and AI training data |
| 🔵 Reduce investment | Generic "what is X" guides | Being answered directly by AI Overviews |

*(Ross Hudgens, [youtube.com/watch?v=8-PS7gR2G0I](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8-PS7gR2G0I), Supermarketers Podcast, collected 22.04.2026)*

**Also:** "The best AI content opportunities in B2B SaaS aren't blog posts. They're the 500 comparison pages, 200 integration pages, and 100 feature pages your competitors haven't built yet." *(Jake Ward, [linkedin.com/in/jakezward](https://www.linkedin.com/in/jakezward/), post: "B2B SaaS and AI Content — The Low-Hanging Fruit", Q1 2026, collected 22.04.2026)*

### STEP 4 — Build the Data Journalism Moat

AI can generate 2,000 words on any topic in 30 seconds. It cannot conduct a survey of 500 CMOs and extract novel insights. That asymmetry is your moat.

**Actions:**
1. Identify an unanswered question in your industry (e.g., *"How long does it actually take B2B SaaS companies to see ROI from content marketing?"*).
2. Commission a survey through SurveyMonkey, Centiment, or YouGov. Budget: $3,000–$6,000 for a statistically significant sample of niche professionals.
3. Publish the findings as a primary report. Then distribute the same data across: blog posts, LinkedIn carousels, PR pitches to industry publications, and inline citations in every relevant article on your site.
4. Repeat quarterly. Each new data point becomes a justified freshness update across your content library.

**Why:** "If you do a data study every quarter, you can update all your other content with that new data. Now you have a new date — and LLMs love new content. A nice cycle that seemingly can perpetuate." *(Ross Hudgens, [youtube.com/watch?v=8-PS7gR2G0I](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8-PS7gR2G0I), collected 22.04.2026)*

### STEP 5 — Implement the 5-Stage Human + AI Content Workflow

Do NOT publish raw AI output. Do NOT write everything manually. The winning system multiplies human expertise with AI efficiency.

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ STAGE 1 — AI RESEARCH (10 min)                                  │
│  Input: Fan-out query cluster from Step 2                       │
│  Action: Feed cluster to Claude/ChatGPT. Extract competing      │
│          entities, common claims, content gaps.                 │
│  Output: Research brief with key claims + sources to cite       │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ STAGE 2 — AI DRAFT (15 min)                                     │
│  Input: Research brief + brand tone guide                       │
│  Action: Generate first draft. Format: TL;DR at top,           │
│          conversational H2/H3 headings, Q&A sections.          │
│  Output: Raw draft ~800–1,200 words                             │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ STAGE 3 — HUMAN EXPERTISE INJECTION (30 min) ← NON-NEGOTIABLE  │
│  Input: Raw draft                                               │
│  Action: Add firsthand experience, brand-specific data,         │
│          expert quotes, case study references.                  │
│          Rewrite any generic-sounding section.                  │
│          Add author bio with credentials + publish date.        │
│  Output: Differentiated, E-E-A-T-compliant draft               │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ STAGE 4 — ENTITY & STRUCTURE QA (10 min)                       │
│  Input: Differentiated draft                                    │
│  Action: Verify heading hierarchy (H1→H2→H3).                  │
│          Confirm TL;DR and Key Takeaway sections exist.         │
│          Check that key entities match what competitors use     │
│          in AI responses (test in AI Studio).                   │
│  Output: Optimized, citable draft                               │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ STAGE 5 — PUBLISH & TRACK (5 min)                               │
│  Input: Optimized draft                                         │
│  Action: Publish with date, author bio, and inline sources.     │
│          Add to prompt tracker (Peak/Profound or manual).       │
│          Set quarterly refresh reminder.                        │
│  Output: Live page + tracking baseline entry                    │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**On TL;DRs:** "Providing a brief TL;DR helps readers and AI quickly grasp key points. AI really likes to cite key takeaway sections." *(Matt Diggity, [youtube.com/watch?v=4GBlHObjOrY](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4GBlHObjOrY), collected 22.04.2026)*

**On human injection:** "AI can write a blog post. It can't share the experience of actually using a product, running a campaign, or serving a customer. E-E-A-T isn't going away — it's becoming more important as AI content floods the web." *(Lily Ray, [linkedin.com/in/lilyray](https://www.linkedin.com/in/lilyray/), post: "E-E-A-T is Non-Negotiable", Q4 2025, collected 22.04.2026)*

### STEP 6 — Build Off-Page Brand Authority Signals

AI models cross-reference brand mentions across trusted third-party sources before citing you. A brand that only exists on its own website is invisible to AI regardless of content quality.

**Actions:**
1. Claim and fully complete every listing: Google Business Profile, G2, Capterra, Trustpilot, LinkedIn, Crunchbase. All information must match your website's About and FAQ pages exactly.
2. Run proactive review campaigns. "The number of reviews and average star rating has a disproportionate amount of control over your AI SEO rankings." *(Matt Diggity, [youtube.com/watch?v=4GBlHObjOrY](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4GBlHObjOrY), collected 22.04.2026)*
3. Use the same brand positioning statement, value proposition, and category descriptor everywhere online. Consistency trains LLMs to associate your brand with the right topics. *(Ross Hudgens citing Rand Fishkin, [youtube.com/watch?v=8-PS7gR2G0I](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8-PS7gR2G0I), collected 22.04.2026)*
4. Invest in digital PR — earn mentions in respected industry publications. "Total number of referring domains is the highest correlation factor for ChatGPT citations." *(Bernard Huang citing SE Ranking study, [youtube.com/watch?v=2Ldce9z_ZuM](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2Ldce9z_ZuM), collected 22.04.2026)*
5. Participate authentically in Reddit communities and on LinkedIn. These platforms are among the most-cited domains in AI responses. "Reddit isn't just a community platform anymore. It's one of the most-cited domains in AI search." *(Kevin Indig, [linkedin.com/in/kevinindig](https://www.linkedin.com/in/kevinindig/), post: "Community as a Signal", Q1 2026, collected 22.04.2026)*

### STEP 7 — Track AI Visibility in Parallel with Traditional Metrics

| Metric | What it measures | Tool |
|--------|----------------|------|
| AI brand mentions | Brand in responses to commercial prompts | Peak, Profound, manual prompt testing |
| AI citations | Your pages cited as AI sources | Ahrefs Brand Radar, Peak |
| Branded search volume | Proxy: users see your brand in AI, then search for you | Google Search Console |
| AI referral traffic | Direct clicks from AI platforms | Google Analytics 4 |

**GA4 setup:** Reports → Acquisition → Traffic Acquisition → Add Comparison → Filter session source/medium → matches regex: `(perplexity|chatgpt|claude|gemini|copilot|you\.com)` *(Matt Diggity, [youtube.com/watch?v=4GBlHObjOrY](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4GBlHObjOrY), collected 22.04.2026)*

**Key mindset shift:** "Traffic is a nice side effect of improving AI visibility, but AI is answering questions not to send you traffic — to give the best response to the end user." *(Bernard Huang, [youtube.com/watch?v=2Ldce9z_ZuM](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2Ldce9z_ZuM), collected 22.04.2026)*

### STEP 8 — Run Quarterly AI Brand Sentiment Audits

Once AI models are citing you, monitor what they say — and actively correct negative sentiment on the pages they cite.

**Actions:**
1. Ask ChatGPT and Gemini: *"What are the weaknesses or limitations of [Your Brand]?"* and *"How does [Your Brand] compare to [Top Competitor]?"*
2. Map which of your pages are being cited for queries where negative sentiment appears.
3. On those pages, add concise FAQ sections that directly address the criticism identified. Be honest — if you're expensive, say who you're right for and who you're not.
4. Re-test the same prompts 4–6 weeks later. Track whether sentiment shifts.

**Source:** Bernard Huang demonstrates this for Clearscope's pricing page — finding AI described the tool as "premium in cost, less accessible for small teams," then adding specific FAQ content on the pricing page defending against that association. *(Bernard Huang, [youtube.com/watch?v=2Ldce9z_ZuM](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2Ldce9z_ZuM), collected 22.04.2026)*

## Part 2: Where Experts Disagree

### Disagreement 1: Is AEO/GEO a genuinely new discipline, or renamed SEO?

**Bernard Huang:** AEO is fundamentally different from SEO. AI models run validation queries with near-zero monthly search volume — queries that traditional keyword tools don't surface. Optimizing for these requires new methodology and new tooling. The target is agent reasoning chains, not user search intent. *(Bernard Huang, [youtube.com/watch?v=2Ldce9z_ZuM](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2Ldce9z_ZuM), collected 22.04.2026)*

**Eli Schwartz:** AEO is a rebrand invented to justify new consulting fees. The fundamentals are unchanged: match content to intent, build genuine authority, make your website the definitive source. "AEO isn't new. It's what good SEO has always been. We just have a new acronym to justify new consulting fees." *(Eli Schwartz, [linkedin.com/in/schwartze](https://www.linkedin.com/in/schwartze/), post: "AEO is Not SEO 2.0", Q4 2025, collected 22.04.2026)*

**My position: Eli is right on strategy; Bernard is right on execution.** Schwartz correctly warns against chasing buzzwords — that leads to wasted resources and shortcuts. The underlying goal (be the most trustworthy, authoritative source) hasn't changed. But Huang's methodology of using AI Studio to reverse-engineer validation queries is genuinely novel and actionable. The right move: ignore the rebrand, apply the new workflow. Call it SEO internally, do Bernard's steps.

---

### Disagreement 2: Should TOFU content be deprioritized or abandoned?

**Ross Hudgens:** TOFU "what is" content is being cannibalized by AI. His cross-client data shows consistent traffic declines for educational content. "If your SEO strategy still revolves around 'what is X' blog posts, you're building for a world that no longer exists. AI answers those. Focus on the pages that convert." *(Ross Hudgens, [youtube.com/watch?v=8-PS7gR2G0I](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8-PS7gR2G0I), collected 22.04.2026)*

**Kevin Indig (implied):** Topical authority — including foundational educational content — is what causes AI models to associate your brand with a category. If you don't define the basic terms in your space, AI learns them from your competitors, making you less likely to be cited in BOFU queries later. "The biggest blocker to AI visibility isn't your content. It's your org chart." *(Kevin Indig, [linkedin.com/in/kevinindig](https://www.linkedin.com/in/kevinindig/), post: "Breaking Silos", Q1 2026, collected 22.04.2026)*

**My position: Keep TOFU for AI training; stop measuring it on traffic.** Ross is right that TOFU won't drive clicks. Kevin is right that TOFU content trains AI to associate your brand with core category concepts. The fix: stop putting TOFU on your content calendar as a traffic play. Measure it instead on AI brand mentions alongside key category terms. Update it quarterly with new data. Keep it alive but don't invest in new TOFU pieces unless they target specific fan-out queries.

---

### Disagreement 3: Concise focused content vs. massive semantic content networks

**Kevin Indig:** Shorter, focused content wins AI citations. "Long-form SEO guides used to be the gold standard. But AI models prefer concise, focused answers they can extract and cite. The shift from 'comprehensive' to 'precise' is real." *(Kevin Indig, [linkedin.com/in/kevinindig](https://www.linkedin.com/in/kevinindig/), post: "Shorter Content Wins in AI Citations", late 2025, collected 22.04.2026)*

**Koray Tugberk Gubur:** Topical authority is built through complete, systematic coverage of every sub-topic and entity. A website is a "network of meaning." Incomplete topical coverage signals weakness regardless of individual page quality. *(Koray Tugberk Gubur, [youtube.com/watch?v=9RNJrGhH7ek](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9RNJrGhH7ek), collected 22.04.2026)*

**My position: Koray sets the architecture; Kevin sets the format.** These aren't contradictory — they operate at different levels. You need Koray's topical maps to decide *what* to publish. You need Kevin's format discipline to decide *how* to write each piece. Build a complete semantic network, but write each node as a concise, extractable 800-word answer — not a 5,000-word mega-guide. The right model: many short articles tightly interlinked, not a few sprawling pillars.

## Part 3: What I Rejected and Why

### Rejected: Seeding brand mentions on Reddit and Quora through fake or bought accounts

**The idea:** Since Reddit is one of the most-cited domains in AI responses, some practitioners recommend creating aged accounts or using vendors to seed positive brand discussions. Bernard Huang mentions community mention monitoring and "thoughtfully contributing" to discussions where your brand or competitors are mentioned. *(Bernard Huang, [youtube.com/watch?v=2Ldce9z_ZuM](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2Ldce9z_ZuM), collected 22.04.2026)*

**Why I rejected it:** Eli Schwartz is direct: "Buying old Reddit accounts to seed mentions of your product isn't SEO strategy. It's spam. And LLMs will get better at detecting it, just like Google got better at detecting link spam." *(Eli Schwartz, [linkedin.com/in/schwartze](https://www.linkedin.com/in/schwartze/), post: "Stop Spamming Reddit", early 2026, collected 22.04.2026)*. More critically: for a B2B SaaS company where trust is a core brand value, being caught astroturfing causes far more damage than any citation benefit justifies. The risk/reward is deeply asymmetric.

**What to do instead:** Have actual team members — product managers, founders, support leads — participate authentically in relevant subreddits. The signal has to be real.

---

### Rejected: Fully autonomous AI content pipelines without a mandatory human review gate

**The idea:** Jake Ward argues that the future of AI SEO tools is autonomous workflows — the system identifies opportunities, writes content, and publishes, with humans reviewing output rather than directing each step. "The next generation of AI SEO tools won't ask you what to write. They'll identify the opportunity, create the content, and publish it — with humans validating quality, not directing every step." *(Jake Ward, [linkedin.com/in/jakezward](https://www.linkedin.com/in/jakezward/), post: "Byword's Evolution", 2025, collected 22.04.2026)*

**Why I rejected it:** Google's Helpful Content system targets content "produced at scale" where the primary goal is ranking rather than informing a human. The standard for surviving this is demonstrable "first-hand expertise and depth of knowledge" — which autonomous pipelines structurally cannot inject. Furthermore, in B2B SaaS, a factually wrong product comparison or an inaccurate feature claim can damage active sales cycles. The efficiency gain does not justify the quality and liability risk.

"Every shortcut in SEO eventually becomes a penalty trigger. The Helpful Content standard didn't go away — it got stricter." *(Lily Ray, [linkedin.com/in/lilyray](https://www.linkedin.com/in/lilyray/), post: "The Helpful Content Standard Still Applies", 2025, collected 22.04.2026)*

**What to do instead:** Automate Stages 1 and 2 of the workflow. Keep Stage 3 (expertise injection) as a mandatory human gate before any content goes live.

## Part 4: My Original Ideas

### Original idea 1: The "AI Intercept" Page Architecture

**The idea:** Create a dedicated section — `/ai-answers/` or `/intelligence/` — structured not for human browsing but to intercept AI validation queries at the moment of fact-checking.

Each page in this section would:
- Follow a strict format: Question → Direct 2-sentence Answer → 3 supporting evidence points with sources
- Be built in clean, minimal HTML with JSON-LD markup for entities — minimizing Koray's "cost of retrieval"
- Target the specific fan-out queries you mapped in Step 2 — the ones with near-zero human search volume but high AI validation frequency
- Contain zero marketing language, no CTAs, no promotional framing — purely factual, cited claims

**Why it could work:** AI models are performing validation queries that look nothing like human search queries. No competitor is writing content optimized for *those* queries because traditional keyword tools don't surface them. By building pages explicitly designed to intercept AI validation — formatted for machine extraction — you create a structural advantage. The AI finds your page, extracts your answer efficiently (low cost of retrieval), and cites you. Traditional SEO pages compete against each other; these pages would initially compete against almost nothing.

**The honest caveat:** If Google extends its "written for search engines, not humans" policies to cover AI-optimized content, this could backfire. This idea requires careful monitoring and should be tested on a small cluster before scaling.

---

### Original idea 2: The Quarterly "AI Brand Reputation Backlog"

**The idea:** Turn the AI Brand Sentiment Audit (Step 8) into a formal product management process. Every quarter, systematically query ChatGPT and Gemini for your brand's weaknesses, limitations, and comparison disadvantages. Treat the output as a ranked product backlog:

1. Tag each negative association by which page is being cited
2. Prioritize patches based on how commercially sensitive the association is
3. Assign ownership (content team writes the FAQ section; product/sales reviews for accuracy)
4. Track resolution: test the same prompt 4 and 8 weeks after the patch is live

**Why it could work:** Bernard Huang demonstrates the core tactic for Clearscope *(Bernard Huang, [youtube.com/watch?v=2Ldce9z_ZuM](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2Ldce9z_ZuM), collected 22.04.2026)*, but as a one-time example. No expert in this research treats it as a *recurring, structured process with ownership and tracking*. Most companies address negative AI sentiment reactively, when a sales rep notices a bad ChatGPT response during a demo. Doing this proactively, on a quarterly cadence with a formal backlog, converts a reactive problem into a systematic brand management advantage.

## Part 5: Weaknesses of This Playbook

**1. The measurement problem is real and currently unsolved.**
Tracking AI citations and "Share of Model" requires relying on third-party tools (Peak, Profound) that sample only a fraction of possible prompts. There is no GA4-equivalent for AI visibility. You cannot cleanly attribute a pipeline opportunity to an AI brand mention. Many organizations will reject this playbook on the grounds that they cannot prove ROI — which is a fair objection.

**2. The fan-out query methodology is inherently unstable.**
The specific sub-queries AI models perform during validation change when models update. The Google AI Studio grounding queries you map today may look different after a model update in 60 days. This means Step 2 cannot be a one-time exercise — it needs to be repeated quarterly, adding ongoing maintenance burden that wasn't required in traditional SEO.

**3. The data journalism recommendation is expensive and slow.**
Commissioning a proper research study costs $3,000–$6,000 and 4–8 weeks of execution time. For early-stage B2B SaaS companies operating with small content budgets, this is not viable. The playbook doesn't offer a credible lower-cost alternative. Customer interview compilations or internally generated usage data could substitute, but these have lower citation authority.

**4. This playbook assumes some brand foundation already exists.**
The tactics in Steps 2, 7, and 8 assume AI models already "know" your brand well enough to have a position on it. For companies starting from near-zero brand recognition, Steps 6 (brand authority) must be completed first before most other steps will produce results. The dependency chain isn't always explicit.

**5. The entire document will need a significant revision by Q4 2026.**
Every recommendation is based on how Gemini and ChatGPT behaved in Q1 2026. Google's AI Mode full rollout, OpenAI's architectural updates, and Perplexity's model changes could render specific tactics obsolete within months. This is a snapshot, not a permanent guide.

## Part 6: Who I Would NOT Recommend Following

### Do not use Julia McCoy as your primary strategic guide.

Of the 10 experts researched, Julia McCoy is the one I would caution against using as a strategic reference for a serious B2B SaaS content operation.

**The conflict of interest:** Julia built Content at Scale — a tool that sells AI content generation at volume — and now runs First Movers, a consulting practice advising companies on AI content adoption. Every recommendation she makes points toward: use more AI, produce more content, move faster. Her financial model depends on that conclusion being correct.

**What's missing from her framework:** Her YouTube content *(Julia McCoy, [youtube.com/watch?v=5K5ItxyPc_k](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5K5ItxyPc_k), collected 22.04.2026)* focuses heavily on efficiency gains and adoption curves. The quality control gates she recommends — "fact-checking, brand voice injection, expert quotes" — are presented as brief checklist items, not as the genuinely difficult, time-consuming process they are. There is no serious treatment of the cases where scaled AI content fails or triggers Helpful Content penalties.

**Contrast with:** Lily Ray *(Lily Ray, [youtube.com/watch?v=-pUMNtq8Bp0](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-pUMNtq8Bp0), collected 22.04.2026)* and Cyrus Shepard *(Cyrus Shepard, [youtube.com/watch?v=ce_4AR5cx8A](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ce_4AR5cx8A), collected 22.04.2026)* — who have no financial stake in AI content tools — both document concrete examples of mass-produced AI content being penalized and of quality standards tightening.

**The bottom line:** Julia's content is useful for understanding AI tooling options and operational workflows. For *strategic* guidance on what to produce, why, and at what risk level — rely on practitioners without a commercial incentive to tell you to produce more AI content.

## Quick Reference Checklist

```
MONTHLY
  □ Test top 10 commercial prompts in ChatGPT + Gemini. Track brand mentions.
  □ Check which pages are cited. Refresh those pages if >90 days old.

QUARTERLY
  □ Run data journalism study. Distribute findings across all relevant content.
  □ Run AI Brand Sentiment Audit. Create patches for negative associations.
  □ Re-run fan-out query mapping in AI Studio. Update content briefs.
  □ Review AI citation tracker trends. Adjust content mix as needed.

EVERY NEW CONTENT PIECE
  □ Fan-out queries mapped before writing begins (not after)
  □ Follows 5-stage workflow — human injection at Stage 3 is mandatory
  □ TL;DR at top, conversational headings, key takeaway section
  □ Author bio with credentials + publish date included
  □ Added to prompt tracker after publishing
  □ Quarterly refresh reminder set

ONGOING
  □ G2/Capterra review campaign active
  □ All brand listings (G2, GBP, LinkedIn, Crunchbase) match website info exactly
  □ Team members participating authentically in 2+ relevant subreddits
  □ LinkedIn thought leadership cadence active (minimum 2x/week)
```
