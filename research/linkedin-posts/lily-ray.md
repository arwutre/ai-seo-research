## Post Theme 1: "E-E-A-T is Non-Negotiable in the AI Era"

**Date Range:** Q4 2025 – Q1 2026
**Source:** LinkedIn

Lily warns against "vibe coding" or over-relying on AI to generate content. She emphasizes that original, human-driven insights are what differentiate content from the massive influx of AI-generated material. Demonstrable Experience remains critical.

> "AI can write a blog post. It can't share the experience of actually using a product, running a campaign, or serving a customer. E-E-A-T isn't going away — it's becoming more important as AI content floods the web."

**Key takeaway:** Human experience and expertise are the ultimate differentiator when AI can produce unlimited commodity content.

## Post Theme 2: "The 'Helpful Content' Standard Still Applies"

**Date Range:** 2025
**Source:** LinkedIn / Conference talks

Lily warns against "risky" GEO tactics — like mass-producing low-quality content, buying old Reddit accounts for artificial consensus, or writing for bots rather than humans. These strategies risk algorithmic demotions.

> "Every shortcut in SEO eventually becomes a penalty trigger. The Helpful Content standard didn't go away — it got stricter. If your content doesn't genuinely help a human, Google and AI will eventually know."

**Key takeaway:** GEO shortcuts are the new link spam — they work temporarily but create long-term risk.

## Post Theme 3: "Brand Building is Now an SEO Strategy"

**Date Range:** Q1 2026
**Source:** LinkedIn

AI models validate credibility through trusted third-party sources (Wikipedia, reputable publications, social conversations). Building a recognized brand that is frequently mentioned across the web is now a critical SEO lever.

> "The most important ranking factor in 2026 isn't a technical signal. It's brand. When AI models look for trustworthy sources, they gravitate toward brands humans already trust."

**Key takeaway:** Brand building (PR, mentions, recognition) is now a core SEO activity, not just a branding exercise.

## Post Theme 4: "GEO is Just Good SEO with a New Name"

**Date Range:** 2025–2026
**Source:** LinkedIn / Substack

Lily maintains a pragmatic view, noting that many "new" GEO tactics are simply fundamental SEO practices (clear headings, intent alignment, structured data) rebranded. She advocates for focusing on sustainable, long-term brand building over quick-fix algorithmic exploits.

> "90% of 'GEO strategies' are just things good SEOs have been doing for years: clear structure, intent matching, schema markup, authoritative sources. Don't let the rebrand fool you."

**Key takeaway:** Most GEO advice is repackaged SEO fundamentals — focus on doing the basics exceptionally well.

---

## Post Theme 5: "Multimodal Content and the AI Visibility Imperative"

**Date Range:** Late 2025
**Source:** LinkedIn / Conference talks

Investing in video, podcasts, and social media is essential. These formats are heavily cited in AI responses and help diversify traffic away from pure reliance on organic search. Lily emphasizes the strategic trade-off of blocking vs. allowing AI crawlers.

> "If you're only creating text content, you're leaving half the AI visibility opportunity on the table. Video, podcasts, images — AI models cite multimodal content at higher rates."

**Key takeaway:** Diversify content formats beyond text — multimodal content gets higher AI citation rates and broader visibility.
