## Post Theme 1: "Brand Search Volume is the New Backlink"

**Date Range:** Q1 2026
**Source:** LinkedIn / YouTube

Matt consistently highlights that Google increasingly uses brand authority as a key indicator of trust. If people are actively searching for your brand name, you are viewed as a more trustworthy source by both Google and AI models.

> "Backlinks used to be the trust signal. Now it's branded searches. If no one is searching for your brand by name, AI models have no reason to cite you."

**Key takeaway:** Building brand awareness that translates to branded search queries is now as important as link building was.

## Post Theme 2: "Don't Abandon SEO — AI Models Rely on It"

**Date Range:** Late 2025
**Source:** LinkedIn

Matt emphasizes that AI models (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google's AI Overviews) heavily prioritize information that is already ranking well on Google. Diverting resources away from traditional SEO can negatively impact your visibility in AI engines.

> "The biggest mistake I see right now? Companies abandoning SEO because 'AI is taking over.' AI is taking its answers from top-ranking pages. If you stop ranking, you stop getting cited."

**Key takeaway:** Traditional SEO is the foundation of AI visibility — ranking well in Google feeds directly into LLM citation.

## Post Theme 4: "The Multi-Channel Imperative"

**Date Range:** Q1 2026
**Source:** LinkedIn

To generate brand search volume and AI visibility, Matt advocates for a multi-channel approach. Don't rely on search as your sole traffic source. Build presence on YouTube, social media, and forums to reinforce your brand across the entire digital ecosystem.

> "Search Everywhere Optimization isn't a buzzword. It's the reality. Your brand needs to show up on YouTube, Reddit, LinkedIn, Twitter — everywhere humans and AI models look for information."

**Key takeaway:** Diversify across channels to build the brand signals that both users and AI models recognize.

## Post Theme 5: "Topical Authority Through AI-Assisted Query Fan-out"

**Date Range:** 2025
**Source:** LinkedIn / YouTube

AI systems utilize "query fan-out" — breaking complex queries into smaller ones — and they rely on sites that provide deep, authoritative coverage of a topic. Matt advises using AI tools to map out all the sub-queries in your niche and systematically covering them.

> "Use AI to understand what AI searches for. Map out every sub-query in your niche. Then build content that answers every single one. That's topical authority in the AI age."

**Key takeaway:** Use AI tools to reverse-engineer the sub-queries that LLMs use for validation, then build content clusters covering all of them.
